About

I am a researcher currently based at the Department of Earth Sciences at the University of Oxford. My background is in Triassic vertebrate palaeontology, and I have broad interests in evolutionary sciences.

At Oxford, I am working as part of the ERC TEMPO project of Prof. Roger Benson. This project aims to investigate large-scale patterns in tetrapod morphological evolution through deep time. My part of the project is focusing on Mesozoic - especially Triassic - taxa, in particular archosaurs and their relatives. I am collecting and working with both 3D surface-scan data and conventional morphometric measurements.

From June 2021 I will be based in the EDDy lab at the University of Liège, investigating the interaction of 3D and 2D quantitative osteological morphology with phylogeny.

Please find links to PDFs of all my publications and data, and teaching resources.
